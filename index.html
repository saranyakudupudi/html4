7 Wonders of World

Taj Mahal, India

The Taj Mahal is a stunning ivory-white marble mausoleum in Agra, India, built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan for his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal. Serving as a timeless symbol of eternal love, considered one of the Seven Wonders of the World, renowned for its perfect symmetry, intricate inlay work, and blend of Indian, Persian, and Islamic architectural styles. Constructed in the mid-17th century, this UNESCO World Heritage site attracts millions of visitors annually to witness its breathtaking beauty and architectural brilliance.

Great Wall of China, China

The Great Wall of China is a series of fortifications in China. They were built across the historical northern borders of ancient Chinese states and Imperial China as protection against various nomadic groups from the Eurasian Steppe.The first walls date to the 7th century BC; these were joined together in the Qin dynasty. Successive dynasties expanded the wall system; the best-known sections were built by the Ming dynasty (1368–1644).

Machu Picchu, Peru

Machu Picchu is an Incan citadel set high in the Andes Mountains in Peru, above the Urubamba River valley. Built in the 15th century and later abandoned, it’s renowned for its sophisticated dry-stone walls that fuse huge blocks without the use of mortar, intriguing buildings that play on astronomical alignments and panoramic views.Its exact former use remains a mystery.

Colosseum, Italy

The Colosseum, or Flavian Amphitheater, is Rome's iconic, massive, elliptical arena, built between 70-80 AD, famous for hosting gladiator contests and public spectacles, symbolizing Roman engineering with its concrete/stone structure, tiered seating for 50,000+, and underground hypogeum, remaining a major landmark and testament to the Roman Empire's power.A marvel of Roman engineering using concrete and stone, featuring arches and different classical column styles.

Chichen Itza, Mexico

Chichen Itza is a famous, sprawling Mayan archaeological site in Mexico's Yucatán Peninsula, known for its iconic Temple of Kukulcan pyramid, a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the New 7 Wonders of the World. This significant pre-Columbian city was a major center for the Maya civilization from around 600 to 1200 AD, featuring structures like the Great Ball Court, Temple of Warriors, and Sacred Cenote, showcasing advanced Maya architecture and astronomy.

Petra, Jordan

Petra is a world-renowned archaeological site in southern Jordan, famous for its stunning rock-cut architecture, especially the iconic Treasury (Al-Khazneh) and Monastery, carved into rose-red sandstone cliffs by the ancient Nabataeans around 300 B.C. as their capital city. A UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World, it was a crucial trade center, showcasing remarkable water engineering and a unique blend of Hellenistic and local styles, requiring days to explore its vast tombs, temples, and canyons.

Christ the Redeemer

Christ the Redeemer statue is the largest Art Deco design ever built in the history of the world. The statue portrays Jesus with arms open, symbolizing welcome and peace. Its construction was proposed in 1850 by priest Pedro Mario in honor of Emperor Pedro II's daughter, Princess Isabel.
